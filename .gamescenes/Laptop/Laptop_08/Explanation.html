!! URGENT SYSTEM ALERT: RANSOMWARE DETECTED - YOUR FILES ARE ENCRYPTED !!

What happened: Your system has been infected with ransomware, a type of malicious software that encrypts your files, making them inaccessible. This infection was triggered by clicking a malicious link in an email.

What this means: All files on your device, including documents, photos, videos, and databases, are now encrypted. You will not be able to open or access these files without a decryption key.

Why this is happening: Ransomware is used by cybercriminals to extort money. They demand a ransom payment in exchange for the decryption key.

What you should do NOW:

  1. DO NOT POWER OFF OR RESTART YOUR COMPUTER. This could worsen the situation.
  2. IMMEDIATELY DISCONNECT from the network (Wi-Fi or Ethernet). This will help prevent the ransomware from spreading to other devices.
  3. DO NOT attempt to modify or repair the encrypted files. This could result in permanent data loss.
  4. Locate the file "DECRYPT_INSTRUCTIONS.txt" on your desktop. This file contains instructions from the attackers.
  5. CONTACT YOUR IT DEPARTMENT IMMEDIATELY. They will assess the situation and determine the best course of action.

Understanding Ransomware:

Ransomware is a serious threat. It often spreads through phishing emails, malicious websites, and infected software downloads. Prevention is key.

How to protect yourself in the future:

  • Be vigilant about emails: Never click on links or open attachments from unknown or suspicious senders.
  • Keep software updated: Regularly update your operating system, antivirus software, and other applications to patch security vulnerabilities.
  • Back up your data: Regularly back up your important files to an external drive or cloud storage. This will allow you to restore your data in case of a ransomware attack.
  • Use strong passwords: Use complex and unique passwords for all your accounts.
  • Be cautious about downloads: Only download software from trusted sources.
  • Implement security awareness training: educate yourself and others about phishing and other cybersecurity threats.

Remember: Paying the ransom is not guaranteed to restore your files and may encourage further criminal activity.
